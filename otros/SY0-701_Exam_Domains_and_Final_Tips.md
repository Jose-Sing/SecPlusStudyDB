## 3. Top 5 Exam Tips

### Tip 1 — Use the Whiteboard/Cheat Sheet
- At in-person centers: a physical dry-erase sheet is provided.
- At home (OnVUE): a digital whiteboard is available in the exam software.
- You may **only** write once the exam clock starts.
- Recommendation: spend the first 1–2 minutes brain-dumping hard-to-remember info (ports, protocols, terms).

### Tip 2 — Skip the Simulations First
- Performance-based questions (simulations) are typically the first 3–5 questions and the hardest.
- Mark them for review, skip them, and complete all multiple-choice questions first.
- Return to simulations at the end, once confidence is built.
- Reported effect: **+5% to +10%** score improvement vs. tackling simulations first.

### Tip 3 — Always Guess if Unsure
- **No penalty for wrong answers** — an unanswered question scores the same as a wrong guess.
- Eliminate as many options as possible, then guess among the remaining ones to improve odds.

### Tip 4 — Pick the Best Time of Day
- Choose a testing time that matches your personal energy pattern (morning vs. night person).
- Avoid testing right after a full work day.
- Arrive (or log in) **20–30 minutes early** to settle in before the clock starts.

### Tip 5 — Be Confident
- Don't schedule the exam until you feel ready — take practice exams first.
- If practice scores are low, review missed topics by rewatching corresponding lessons, then retest.
- Understand **why** an answer is right/wrong — don't memorize the answer key (real exam questions will differ in wording but test the same concepts).
- Additional practice exams available via Udemy (diontraining.com/udemy) — two sets of 6 full-length exams (1,200+ questions total).
